Pesci

benjamin
2023-11-10

Description

Pesci is a Logic Challenge written by benjamin for the 2023 ITIS Leonardo Da Vinci Pre-Test for the CyberSecurity's Course. Was only solved by few people. The description of the challenge is as follow:

A pet store just bought a pair of fish. The owner knows that he will have to wait 1 year for the pair to reach maturity and be able to reproduce, giving birth to a new pair of fish. All the pairs then, after 1 year to reach maturity, will reproduce in turn, each generating a new pair of fish. Knowing that 2 pairs of fish will die in the third year, how many fish will there be in total at the beginning of the fifth year?

Possible answers:

A) 6

B) 8

C) 16

D) 24

A) 32

Solution

The correct answer is C)

In fact, it is enough to double the pairs each year after the first, bearing in mind that in the third year 2 couples die. Therefore:

1 year: 1 pair of fish that must reach maturity to reproduce (21 = 2 fish)

2 year: 2 pairs of fish, as the first pair spawned a second pair of fish (22 = 4 fish)

3 year: 4 pairs of fish â€“ 2 pairs = 2 pairs of fish, as the two pairs generate a pair of fish each, so we have 4 pairs. But we know that 2 die, so only 2 pairs remain alive (23 - 22 = 4 fish)

4 year: 4 pairs of fish, as the two pairs spawn a pair of fish each, so we have 4 pairs (23 = 8 fish)

5 year: 8 pairs of fish, as the 4 pairs spawn one pair of fish each (24 = 16 fish)
